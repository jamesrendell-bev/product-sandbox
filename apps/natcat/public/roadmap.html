How a risk flows through the system — and what's built

One submission's journey from the underwriter's desk to a bound risk or a referral. June 2026.
● BUILT & LIVE ● PoC BUILT — research & calibration needed ● TO BUILD ◌ data roadmap note
1

Location input USER

The underwriter enters a single location (address + TIV + occupancy) or uploads a schedule / bordereau from Excel.

2

AI Ingestion Tool BUILT & LIVE

Cleans, standardises and geo-locates every row — a messy 10,000-location schedule becomes structured, lat/long-tagged data in minutes.

Owner: built by Raghav · already in production.
3

BirdsEyeView hazard model (CERA® API) BUILT & LIVE Hail & SCS ≈ year-end 2026

For each location, returns the full return-period distribution per peril — not a score, the whole curve.

Perils live: Flood (FATHOM), Tropical Cyclone, Earthquake, US Tornado, Wildfire · Endpoints: lite (fast screen) · daily (full curve) · batch (whole books).
4

Vulnerability model PoC — RESEARCH & CALIBRATION

Open-source, peer-reviewed damage curves convert hazard intensity into a damage ratio for the building — tuned by construction, occupancy, year built and roof.

Sources: JRC flood · Emanuel/Eberenz cyclone · GEM earthquake · wildfire conditional-damage. Needs: science-team calibration of the unit conversions & curves.
5

AAL engine PoC — TO PRODUCTISE

Hazard curve × damage curve × TIV → per-peril AAL, a total NatCat AAL, and a confidence score that reflects how much data the underwriter supplied.

Output mirrors the policy: Section 1 (Material Damage) + Section 2 (Business Interruption) = total. Methodology paper + validated calculator already exist.
6

Underwriting layer — authority & triage TO BUILD

The risk is checked against underwriting rules set by a super-user — the manager, or the capacity provider under a binding authority (e.g. Hudson for a US coverholder).

Rules: max TIV · permitted construction/occupancy · excluded zones · per-peril hazard frequency limits · AAL ceilings · minimum terms · rate floors. Every breach names the rule that fired.
7

Portfolio checks — accumulation TO BUILD

The new risk is tested against the in-force book: zone aggregates, PML / tail capital, and whether this risk pushes a peak zone over the limit.

Outputs: Cresta-zone aggregation · 1-in-100/250 PML · 1-in-200 TVaR · marginal impact of the new risk. True event-based PML needs catalogue footprints from data science.

8 · Decision — inside or outside authority?

Everything above resolves to one call, with the reasons listed.

▼ Inside authority — bind

Auto-underwrite with guided terms TO BUILD

The system proposes deductibles, sublimits and layers within the rules, prices a capital-aware technical premium, and assembles the wording schedule.

▼ Outside authority — refer

One-click referral report TO BUILD

A branded placement document — risk summary, the rule that fired, hazard & AAL detail, proposed terms, price — goes straight to the capacity provider. No hand-written email.

9

Export for independent modelling TO BUILD

Cleaned, bound risks export to RMS EDM or Verisk CEDE format — so the book can be run through the big cat models independently, with zero re-keying.

Why it matters: capacity providers and reinsurers ask for exactly these formats — this turns a data-cleaning chore into a one-click handoff.
